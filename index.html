Introduction

What is Information Security?

To understand information security in healthcare, we have to first understand what information security is.
Information security refers to the processes and tools designed and deployed to protect sensitive business information from modification, disruption, destruction, and inspection. While information security protects all forms of information, cybersecurity, a subset of information security, protects all forms of digital information including computers, handheld devices, cloud, and networks.

In healthcare, information security is achieved by ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information. As a growing trend for digitalization, there is also a growing amount of threats that can target health information.

Why is Information Security Important?

Identifying the Importance of Information Security

Technology is everchanging, and in healthcare, information security is important in order to keep patient information safe and secured. As clinics and hospitals using paper medical records start adopting new electronic health record systems, it is important to identify the risks and practice the proper procedure in protecting electronic health information.

Implementations of EHR or electronic health record, it introduces risks to health information. An EHR is an eletronic record of health related information of an individual that conforms to nationally recognized interoperability standards and that can be created, managed, and consulted by authorized clinicians and staff across more than one health care organization.

Safeguards are solutions and tools used to implement security policies. It can be administrative, physical, or technical. Below are some examples of each safeguard.

  • Continual risk assessment of HIT environment
  • Detailed processes for viewing and administering electronic health information
  • Employee training on the use of HIT to appropriately protect electronic health information
  • appropriately reporting security breaches and ensuring continued HIT operations

  • Office alarm systems
  • Locked offices containing computing equipment that stores electronic health information
  • Security guards

  • Securely configured computer equipment
  • Certified applications and technologies that store or exchange electronic health information
  • Encryption of electronic health information
  • HIT backup capabilities

    • It is important for both patients, workforce members, CISO (chief information security officer), and vendor/market suppliers to communicate with each other to ensure information is not to be compromised.

    Patients need to understand the privacy and security polices how to securely communicate with their healthcare providers in order to keep their information private and secure.

    Regular security awareness training should be provided to workforce members so they are aware of threats and what to do incase of actual security incidents.

    Terminology

    Information security - The protection of information and information systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.

    Cybersurity - Protection of digial information, including computers, handheld devices, cloud, and netowrks.

    Confidentiality - The property that electronic health information is not made available or disclosed to unauthorized persons or processes.

    Integrity - The property that electronic health information have not been altered or destroyed in an unauthorized manner.

    Availability - The property that electronic health information is accessible and useable upon demand by an authorized person.

    EHR - The systemized collection of patient and population electronically stored health information in a digital format.

    Safeguards - Policies, procedures, and physical and technical measures that protect patients' health information.
